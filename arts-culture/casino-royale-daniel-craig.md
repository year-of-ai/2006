# Casino Royale (2006): Daniel Craig Bond Reboot

**Category:** Arts & Culture
**Key figures:** Daniel Craig (James Bond), Martin Campbell (director), Barbara Broccoli and Michael G. Wilson (producers), Neal Purvis, Robert Wade, and Paul Haggis (screenwriters), Judi Dench (M)

## Summary

Casino Royale, released on November 17, 2006 in the United States, is the twenty-first film in Eon Productions' James Bond series and the first to star Daniel Craig as MI6 agent James Bond. The film is the third screen adaptation of Ian Fleming's 1953 debut Bond novel of the same name, and it was directed by Martin Campbell, who had previously helmed GoldenEye (1995). Produced on a budget of approximately $150 million, it depicts a younger, less experienced Bond earning his 00 status — a deliberate franchise reboot following the decision by Eon Productions to abandon the continuity established across the previous twenty films. Craig's casting, announced in October 2005, generated immediate public controversy: critics objected that the blond, 5-foot-10 actor did not conform to Ian Fleming's tall, dark-haired description of Bond, and an online campaign titled "danielcraigisnotbond.com" attracted thousands of signatures. The film grossed approximately $599 million worldwide, making it the highest-grossing Bond film to that date and the fourth-highest-grossing film of 2006 globally; it holds a 95% approval rating on Rotten Tomatoes.

## Significance

Casino Royale reinvented a franchise that had grown formulaic, replacing the gadgetry and irony of preceding installments with psychological realism and physical grit, and Craig's performance silenced pre-release detractors to earn near-universal critical acclaim. The film established a template for darker, character-driven blockbusters — an influence visible across action and superhero cinema in the years that followed — and is widely credited with saving the Bond franchise for a new generation of audiences.
